Obituary: Maureen Guthman Uterine Sarcoma

Maureen, a prominent figure in the media industry and a beacon of diversity and inclusion succumbed to uterine sarcoma on November 30, 2022.

She was 60 years old and a proud resident of New York City, where she spent most of her life.

Her demise leaves a profound void; she was a loving and cherished wife, sister, aunt, and friend. Maureen was shaped by her experiences, most notably by the early deaths of her parents, who passed away when she was only 16.

Nevertheless, she approached life with an indomitable spirit and found grace amidst adversity. She was a living testament to the power of resilience; a trait mirrored in her professional journey.

After graduating from Hunter College, Maureen began her career as an intern at Viacom.

Over time, she climbed the ranks to become the Senior Vice President of Content for BET Networks, showcasing her dedication and pioneering vision.

A constant advocate for diversity, she worked tirelessly to ensure the representation of people of color, women, and the LGBTQ+ community on screen. In addition to her career in the media, Maureen was a woman of many talents and interests.

She was an accomplished musician and an ardent lover of books and movies. Her zest for life took her to different parts of the world, and her intellectual curiosity drove her to continual personal and professional growth.

Maureen is survived by her wife, Andrea Meyerson, her mother, Christine, her brother Edward, sisters Karen (Donald), Joanne (Nodran), Jillian, and Jennifer (Greg), as well as 12 beloved nieces and nephews.

She is predeceased by her mother, Bernice Guthman (nee Thornton), her father, Andre J. Guthman, her brothers, Andre Guthman and Raymond Guthman, and her niece Andrea Guthman.

BET Executive Maureen Guthman Death

Maureen’s passing marks a significant loss in the media industry. As the Senior Vice President of Content for BET Networks, Maureen was a transformative leader who left an indelible mark on the field.

Her commitment to diversity and inclusivity shaped the network’s programming and influenced the broader industry. Maureen received numerous awards throughout her career, demonstrating her contributions to the entertainment world.

She mentored many, sharing her wisdom, experiences, and unwavering support with those privileged enough to work under her leadership. Many admired her sharp intellect and wit, and will be remembered fondly by those who knew her.

Maureen’s impact extended beyond her professional work. She was a philanthropist who cared deeply about animal rescue and music education, urging people to express their condolences by donating to organizations like BARRK Animal Rescue.

A memorial service was held in honor of Maureen at the Ascent Lounge at the Deutsche Bank Center on Monday, December 12, 2022.

Friends, family, and colleagues gathered to pay their respects to a woman who lived her life with impact.

In memory of Guthman, let us strive to honor her legacy by promoting diversity and inclusion in every walk of life.

She may have departed from this world, but her spirit and the principles she stood for will continue to inspire many generations.
